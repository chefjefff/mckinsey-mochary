# How We Do Work — the foundational triad

**The rule:** Three skills are the foundational operating system for every agent and every main-thread task. They are always on. They are not optional. They are the work itself.

- **`/problem-solving`** — How we **THINK**. 7-step Bulletproof Problem Solving framework (Conn & McLean).
- **`/work-plan`** — How we **PLAN**. The workplanning method (Step 4 of /problem-solving, extracted).
- **`/mochary-method`** — How we **OPERATE**. Matt Mochary's operating system (Top Goal, DRI, RAPID, feedback, meetings, emotional regulation).

**Why:** Most work fails at one of three layers: thinking, planning, or operating. Plans fail because they're logistics-led (work-plan gap). Decisions fail because they're advocacy-led (mochary gap). Analyses fail because they're unstructured (problem-solving gap). The triad forces rigor at all three layers.

## How to apply

### The triad in use

| Skill | What it gives you | Use it when |
|-------|------|------|
| `/problem-solving` | Analytical structure — MECE trees, hypothesis-driven analysis, cleaving frames, bias-fighting, synthesis, pyramid storytelling | Any complex question, strategic diagnosis, "should we do X" decision, root cause analysis, competitive analysis, uncertainty management |
| `/work-plan` | Execution structure — hypothesis, knock-out, SCR, funnel math, 6-column plan with DRIs and times, kill list, risks, checklist | Every scoping, projection, sprint plan, strategic output, weekly priorities, event plan, proposal, review |
| `/mochary-method` | Operating discipline — Top Goal, Zone of Genius, DRI, RAPID, Issue/Proposed Solution, Clean Escalation, feedback, meetings, Fear/Anger regulation | Every decision, meeting, priority call, feedback moment, escalation, team dynamic, 1:1 |

### The three-question test (run before handing over any output)

1. **Thinking:** Is the analytical structure clear? Problem defined, tree MECE, hypotheses testable, synthesis visible? (`/problem-solving`)
2. **Planning:** Does every activity tie to a measurable outcome with a named owner and a date + time? (`/work-plan`)
3. **Operating:** Is there a clear DRI for every action? Are priorities framed as issues with proposed solutions? Is the framing facts-not-advocacy? (`/mochary-method`)

If any answer is no, rewrite.

### Anti-patterns to flag and fix

- Logistics checklists disguised as plans (work-plan gap)
- Goals without hypotheses (work-plan + problem-solving gap)
- Tasks without single named DRIs (mochary gap)
- Vague due dates ("next week", "by Friday") (work-plan gap)
- Outputs that aren't dummied (work-plan gap)
- Missing knock-out — undefined critical path (work-plan gap)
- Missing post-execution workplan — follow-up where outcomes are made or lost (work-plan gap)
- "Boil the ocean" before defining the problem (problem-solving gap)
- Advocacy framed as analysis, fear/anger driving a priority (mochary gap)
- Issue without proposed solution ("just raising it") (mochary gap)
- Meetings without agendas, decisions without RAPID clarity (mochary gap)
- Generic narrative instead of pyramid-structured argument (problem-solving gap)

### When to invoke

**Always:** event plans, weekly priorities, proposals, monthly reviews, campaign strategy, monthly content batches, channel readouts with recommendations, RFI responses, outreach sequences, SEO pipelines, quarterly planning, roadmap memos, team retrospectives, 1:1s, decision escalations.

**When in doubt:** Invoke. A 10-minute structure investment saves 10 hours of wasted work.

**When NOT to invoke:** Pure production tasks with a defined spec (single tweet, single compliance review, single report to a known format), single-step reads, conversational exchanges.
