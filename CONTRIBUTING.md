# Contributing

This repo is a focused packaging of three existing frameworks. Contributions are welcome — but please read this first.

## What I'm looking for

- **Case studies.** Real before/after examples of the triad applied to your work. Event plans, proposals, sprint plans, monthly reviews, anything. Submit as a PR to `examples/`.
- **Platform adapters.** The skills are just markdown. If you make them work cleanly on Cursor, Cline, Continue, or another agent system, submit a PR with a brief adapter guide in `docs/` (or however makes sense).
- **Translations.** Not just language — translations for adjacent roles (product managers, consultants, founders, engineers). Submit as variants in `skills/<skill>/` or as adapter docs.
- **Corrections.** If I got Conn, McLean, or Mochary wrong — fix it. Accurate attribution matters more than anything else in this repo.
- **Sharper framings.** If the anti-patterns list or the 3-question test can be sharpened without losing precision, go for it.

## What I'm NOT looking for

- **Expansions of the skills into proprietary frameworks.** The skills are distillations of existing books and open methods. Adding "Jeffrey's 12-step enhancement" defeats the point. Keep the sources clean.
- **Marketing copy.** This is a working tool, not a brand asset.
- **Integration with paid tools.** The whole point is portability. Keep dependencies at zero.
- **Feature creep.** Three skills. Not four. Not seven.

## How to contribute

1. Open an issue describing what you want to change and why. This saves both of us time if the change isn't a fit.
2. For small edits (typos, clarity), just open a PR directly.
3. For new case studies, follow the format in `examples/case-study-event-plan.md` — before diagnostic + after diagnostic + what changed, structured as a narrative.
4. For new platform adapters, show the actual install flow on that platform and what "working" looks like.

## Style notes

- Write for a reader who has 90 seconds and a cup of coffee. Not 30 minutes.
- Use concrete examples, not abstract frameworks.
- No jargon without definition. If you're using a term from Conn/McLean or Mochary, link to the source.
- No emoji inflation. One emoji per section header at most.
- Avoid "powerful", "revolutionary", "game-changing" — and other words that don't survive editing.

## Attribution

Every PR that meaningfully adds content gets credited in the commit history and (if substantial) in the README. If the change is a significant improvement, I'll tag you in a LinkedIn post when I share the update.

## Code of conduct

Be the kind of person a McKinsey engagement manager would want in their study team:
- Direct but respectful
- Opinions held loosely
- Evidence over rhetoric
- Help the team's answer win, not yours
