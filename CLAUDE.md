## 🧭 HOW WE DO WORK (foundational triad — read first, every time)

**Three skills form the operating foundation for every agent, every skill, and every main-thread task. They are always on. They are not optional. They are the work itself.**

### The Triad

| Skill | Role | When |
|-------|------|------|
| **`/problem-solving`** | How we **THINK** | Every complex question, analysis, strategic diagnosis, or "should we do X?" decision |
| **`/work-plan`** | How we **PLAN** | Every scoping, projection, sprint plan, or "what are we actually doing?" output |
| **`/mochary-method`** | How we **OPERATE** | Every team interaction, decision moment, priority call, meeting, feedback, and emotional regulation check |

Together they cover: analytical rigor (problem-solving), execution discipline (work-plan), and operating excellence (mochary-method). **Any non-trivial output carries evidence of all three.**

### `/problem-solving` — How we THINK

7-step Bulletproof Problem Solving (Conn & McLean):

1. Define the problem (SMART+, porpoise to refine)
2. Disaggregate with MECE logic trees
3. Prioritize (impact × ability-to-move 2x2)
4. Build workplan (hands to /work-plan)
5. Analyze (heuristics first: Occam, 80:20, Rule of 72, 5 Whys → big guns if needed)
6. Synthesize (pyramid, visualize, cross-cutting insights)
7. Tell the story (SCR, lead with resolution)

**Cleaving frames:** ROIC, price/volume, principal/agent, assets/options, supply/demand, regulate/incent, incidence/severity.

**Bias fighting:** obligation to dissent, multiple trees, "what would you have to believe?", pre-mortem, distributed voting.

### `/work-plan` — How we PLAN

Required elements for every plan, projection, or strategic output:

1. **Hypothesis** — "If we do X, we get Y because Z"
2. **Knock-out analysis** — the one check that kills the project if it fails. Sequence FIRST.
3. **SCR one-day answer** — Situation / Complication / Resolution
4. **Funnel or projection** with explicit conversion math (not just targets)
5. **6-column workplan** — Issue / Hypothesis / Analysis / Owner / Output / Due (date + time)
6. **Kill list** — what we're explicitly NOT doing
7. **Risk register** — likelihood × impact × mitigation
8. **Pre-launch checklist** before locking

### `/mochary-method` — How we OPERATE

- **Top Goal** — 2 uninterrupted hours/day on the single highest priority, calendar-blocked
- **Zone of Genius** — spend time only on what only you can do; delegate/kill the rest
- **DRI** — every task, deliverable, decision has one Directly Responsible Individual
- **RAPID** — Recommend, Agree, Perform, Input, Decide
- **Issue / Proposed Solution** — state facts, story, proposed action with DRI + due. Never bring an issue without a proposed solution.
- **Clean Escalation** — facts + options + tradeoffs, not advocacy
- **Fear & Anger regulation** — name it, feel it, let it go. Don't operate from fear/anger state.
- **Feedback model** — "I noticed X → I felt Y → I'd love Z"

### The 3-question test (run before handing over any output)

1. **Thinking:** Is the analytical structure clear? Problem defined, tree MECE, hypotheses testable, synthesis visible? (`/problem-solving`)
2. **Planning:** Does every activity tie back to a measurable outcome with a named owner and a date + time? (`/work-plan`)
3. **Operating:** Is there a clear DRI for every action? Are priorities framed as issues with proposed solutions? Is the framing facts-not-advocacy? (`/mochary-method`)

If any answer is no, rewrite.

### Anti-patterns we do not accept

- ❌ Logistics checklists disguised as plans
- ❌ Goals without hypotheses
- ❌ Tasks without single named owners (DRI)
- ❌ Due dates without times
- ❌ Outputs that aren't dummied
- ❌ Equal weighting — no knock-out identified
- ❌ Missing post-execution workplan
- ❌ "Boil the ocean" before defining the problem
- ❌ Advocacy framed as analysis
- ❌ Meetings without agendas, decisions without RAPID clarity
- ❌ Issue without proposed solution ("just raising it")
- ❌ Generic narrative instead of pyramid-structured argument

### When the triad applies

Every strategic output: event plans, weekly priorities, proposals, campaign strategy, monthly reviews, content batches, outreach sequences, roadmap memos, quarterly planning, team retrospectives, 1:1s, decision escalations.

### When it does not apply

Pure production tasks with a defined spec (single tweet, single review, single report to a known format), single-step edits, conversational exchanges.

### Why this matters

Most work fails at one of three layers: thinking, planning, or operating. Plans fail because they're logistics-led (work-plan gap). Decisions fail because they're advocacy-led (mochary gap). Analyses fail because they're unstructured (problem-solving gap). The triad forces rigor at all three layers before work becomes expensive to fix.
