# The Mochary Method

*Source: Matt Mochary, "The Great CEO Within" + open-source Mochary Method Curriculum*

Matt Mochary coaches the top CEOs in Silicon Valley (Sam Altman, Brian Armstrong, Naval Ravikant). He open-sourced his entire method. This skill codifies it into modular components you can pull individually.

---

## Component 1: Personal Productivity

### Top Goal

The single most important habit in the method.

- Schedule **2 uninterrupted hours daily**, early in the day
- Work ONLY on your single highest priority -- nothing else
- Calendar-block it. Treat it like an external meeting you can't move.
- Use an accountability partner (text them your Top Goal each morning, confirm completion each evening)

**Why it works:** External requests expand to fill all available time. Without a protected block, your most important work never happens.

### Energy Audit

Print your calendar for the past 2 weeks. Color-code every hour:

- **Green** = you had MORE energy at the end than the start
- **Red** = you had LESS energy at the end

**Target:** 75-80% green time.

For red activities: delegate, eliminate, or restructure. If you can't eliminate, batch them into a single day so they don't contaminate green days.

### Zone of Genius

Four zones (from Gay Hendricks):

| Zone | Definition | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Incompetence** | Others are better than you | Delegate immediately |
| **Competence** | You're adequate, but others match you | Delegate |
| **Excellence** | You're better than most, BUT you don't love it | Dangerous -- delegate this too |
| **Genius** | Uniquely skilled AND you love it | Spend 75-80% of time here |

The trap is Zone of Excellence. You're great at it, people praise you for it, but it drains you. That's the zone to escape.

### GTD (Getting Things Done)

Maintain these lists in a single trusted system:

1. **Next Actions** -- priority tasks by context
2. **Waiting For** -- items delegated to others (with dates)
3. **Someday/Maybe** -- future possibilities
4. **Agenda** -- topics for recurring meetings (one list per person/meeting)
5. **Projects** -- multi-step serial tasks
6. **Goals** -- vision and OKRs reference

### Inbox Zero

- Check email only **2-3 times daily** (batch processing)
- Sub-2-minute items: respond immediately
- Everything else: move to Next Actions list
- Process to empty every session
- Eliminates context-switching tax

### Gratitude Practice

Write one thing you're grateful for each morning. Shifts baseline mindset. Mochary's claim: people perform best when having fun and feeling good about themselves.

## Component 2: Emotional Regulation

### Fear and Anger Give Bad Advice

The foundational Mochary principle. Never make decisions or take action while in fear or anger.

**Fear:**
- Grips you and leads to exaggerated predictions
- Makes you see worst-case as most likely
- When feeling fear: pause, check with someone NOT experiencing it
- They'll help you see the situation accurately

**Anger:**
- Not a base emotion -- it's a cover for pain
- Shifting from anger = acknowledging and FEELING the underlying pain directly
- Don't project it outward. Sit with it.
- Once the pain is felt, anger dissolves and clear thinking returns

**Practice:**
1. Notice the emotion (name it)
2. Pause -- do NOT act
3. Breathe, feel the underlying sensation
4. Check with a neutral party
5. Only then decide

### Conscious Leadership

From the Conscious Leadership Group (referenced heavily in the curriculum):

- **Above the line:** Open, curious, committed to learning
- **Below the line:** Closed, defensive, committed to being right

Check yourself: "Am I above or below the line right now?" If below, don't make decisions. Shift first.

## Component 3: Meetings & Team Operations

### Meeting Principles

1. **Pre-write everything.** Anyone raising a topic writes it up BEFORE the meeting, including a proposed solution. No verbal-only agenda items.
2. **Writing over talking.** Writing scales; spoken words are ephemeral. Written proposals circulated pre-meeting = 3x more productive meetings.
3. **Leader speaks LAST.** Gather all opinions before revealing yours. Prevents anchoring bias.
4. **Silent input first.** Have team write thoughts before anyone speaks. Captures introverted perspectives, prevents dominant voice bias.
5. **5-minute rule.** If a decision can't be reached in 5 minutes of discussion, escalate to RAPID framework.

### Meeting Cadence (Recommended Weekly)

| Meeting | Purpose | Format |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **1:1s** | Coaching + accountability | See Component 4 |
| **Leadership team** | Cross-functional decisions | Issue/Proposed Solution |
| **CEO open office hour** | Accessibility | Drop-in |
| **All-hands** | Transparency + alignment | Updates + Q&A |
| **Social event** | Culture + connection | Informal |

**Critical rule:** All meeting notes published company-wide. Attendance should never be required for information access.

### No-Meeting Days

- 1 day: no internal meetings
- 1 day: no external meetings
- 3 days: no meetings at all (aspirational target)

Arrive 15 min early to external meetings. Wrap internal meetings 5-10 min early.

### DRI (Directly Responsible Individual)

Assign ONE person as DRI for every:
- Metric
- Goal
- Project
- Meeting
- Decision

Multiple owners = no owner. If six people are debating in Slack for three days, you don't have a DRI.

### Areas of Responsibility (AOR)

Master document listing ALL company functions with designated DRI. Update as roles shift. Cross-train a backup for each critical role. No single points of failure.

### Agreement Tracker

Impeccable agreements are:
- **Precisely defined** (not vague)
- **Written** (not verbal)
- **Fully agreed to** by all relevant parties
- **Tracked** with DRI + due date

If a commitment can't be met, notify immediately -- don't wait until the deadline. "Verbal agreements are not impeccable."

### OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)

**Structure:** 3 objectives x 3 key results each. Cascade: Company -> Department -> Team -> Individual.

- **Objective:** "Where do we want to go?" (not necessarily measurable, inspirational)
- **Key Result:** "How do we know we're getting there?" (measurable, time-bound)

**Implementation:**
- Leadership brings ideas pre-meeting
- Better to let individuals create their own (increases emotional investment)
- Track quarterly via traffic-light system (Green/Yellow/Red)
- Review progress weekly

### KPIs

- Track 5-6 most significant metrics
- Make visible to entire team (dashboard on screen)
- Include counter-metrics to prevent over-optimization
- Review regularly

## Component 4: 1:1 Format & Feedback

### 1:1 Meeting Structure

The Mochary 1:1 is NOT a status update. It's coaching.

**Format:**
1. **Report's agenda first** (their topics, not yours)
2. **Accountability check:** Did you complete what you committed to?
3. **Coaching:** What's the current health? What's going well? What's not going well?
4. **Issue/Proposed Solution:** For anything not going well, present the issue + a proposed solution
5. **Commitments:** What will you do before next 1:1?

The manager's job in a 1:1 is to LISTEN, not to direct.

### Feedback Framework (Nonviolent Communication adapted)

**Giving feedback:**
1. **Ask permission** -- "Can I share some feedback?"
2. **State the behavior** -- facts only, no judgment ("When you arrived 10 minutes late to the client meeting...")
3. **State how it made you feel** -- use actual emotions ("...I felt anxious and embarrassed")
4. **Share your story** -- your interpretation ("My story is that the client thought we don't value their time")
5. **Make a specific request** -- positive action ("Would you commit to arriving 5 minutes early to client meetings?")
6. **Confirm acceptance** -- "Does that work for you?"

**Critical rule:** NEVER deliver negative feedback via email, text, or voicemail. Always in person or live video.

### Seeking Feedback: The 4 A's

1. **Ask** for it (regularly, proactively)
2. **Acknowledge** it (repeat back what they said to confirm understanding)
3. **Appreciate** it (thank them -- even if you disagree)
4. **Act** on it (implement or explain why not)

### Appreciation

- Only correct response to a compliment: "Thank you." Full stop.
- Never deflect, downplay, or over-explain.
- Reserve 1 hour weekly for intentional appreciation outreach.
- When you catch gratitude toward someone, communicate it to them.

### Fact vs. Story Separation

Distinguish observable facts from interpretations:
- **Fact:** "You submitted the report on Thursday" (observable)
- **Story:** "You don't care about deadlines" (judgment)

Present facts first. Label stories explicitly as "my story is..." Reduces defensiveness, enables clearer problem-solving.

## Component 5: Decision-Making

### Issue / Proposed Solution Format

The standard unit of decision-making in the Mochary Method:

1. **Facts** -- physical actions that have occurred, no judgment
2. **Story** -- thoughts, opinions, interpretations
3. **Proposed Solution** -- very specific action items with DRI + due date

**Rule:** You cannot say "I don't know." You must present a guess, phrased boldly. Discussion happens around a concrete proposal, not a blank page.

### RAPID Framework (from Bain & Company)

Use when a team is too large for easy consensus, or when 5-minute discussion doesn't resolve it.

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **R** (Recommend) | The person who proposed the issue and solution |
| **A** (Agree) | People whose input MUST be incorporated |
| **P** (Perform) | People who will execute the decision |
| **I** (Input) | Senior stakeholders whose departments are affected |
| **D** (Decide) | Final decision-maker |

### Reversibility Principle

- **Irreversible decisions:** CEO decides. Take time, deliberate carefully.
- **Reversible decisions:** Delegate to reports. Decide quickly, iterate.

Most decisions are reversible. Default to speed.

### Clean Escalation

When escalating a decision:
- Present **facts without advocacy** -- let the decision-maker choose
- No spin, no politics, no "selling" your preferred outcome
- Normalizes escalation and removes shame
- Format: "Here are the facts. Here are two options. Here are the tradeoffs. What do you want to do?"

### Buy-In vs. Speed

Buy-in and speed of decision are inversely correlated.

**Three methods (ranked by buy-in, lowest to highest):**
1. **Decide -> Tell -> Answer questions** (fastest, lowest buy-in)
2. **Straw man approach** (optimal): Create proposal -> Share -> Invite feedback -> Discuss -> Decide
3. **Open discussion:** No proposal, brainstorm from scratch (slowest, highest buy-in)

Default to #2 for most decisions. Use #1 only for trivial/reversible ones. Use #3 only for high-stakes, values-level decisions.

### Avoiding Groupthink

- Have team write votes/thoughts BEFORE leader shares perspective
- Let junior staff speak and question first
- Titles influence answers -- neutralize hierarchy in decision sessions

## Component 6: Hiring, Firing & People

### Hiring

**Sourcing:** Contact your top 10 most talented people weekly. Ask each for 3 referrals. Document all candidates in ATS.

**Interview focus:**
- Ask: "What are the 3 most important issues for you to solve in the first 90 days?"
- Ask why they left previous roles (promoted out? recruited? fired?)
- Share a written 90-day roadmap of what the role requires -- confirm excitement

**Rule:** Hire only A-players who fit both job requirements AND culture. Keep pre-PMF team under 6.

### Onboarding (More Critical Than Recruiting)

Most companies neglect this. Mochary's protocol:
- Create comprehensive checklist + video of required knowledge
- Schedule first day 2 hours after normal start (more people present)
- Assign a **buddy** for first 2 weeks
- Daily 15-minute check-ins with buddy
- Don't "cannonball" into a new leadership role -- spend initial weeks observing

### Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

For underperformers:
1. Document written PIP with milestones at 7-, 30-, 60-, 90-day marks
2. Weekly progress meetings
3. Termination decision at 30 days if milestones missed
4. Provides legal protection and small chance of improvement

### Firing with Grace

- Publicly praise the person's contributions
- Take ownership: "I couldn't match your skills to our needs"
- Do NOT blame or criticize
- Absolve them of responsibility
- Help misaligned employees find better fits (if values diverge, facilitate exit)

### Compensation & Politics Prevention

- Create written policies for raises, promotions, compensation
- Apply consistently -- never reward lobbying
- Implement Grade Level Planning (GLP) at 25-50 employees
- Every position at every seniority level has specific compensation + advancement criteria
- Visible to all staff

## Component 7: Company Culture & Transparency

### Culture Definition

By 30 employees, culture exists -- codify it, don't create it.

**Method:** Identify 3-5 non-leadership staff who exemplify desired behaviors. Name them and the specific behaviors. Those behaviors = your values.

**Alternative (small teams):** Complete: "You can make all decisions if you..."

### Accountability + Coaching + Transparency

Three organizational requirements at every level:

| Pillar | Definition |
|--------|-----------|
| **Accountability** | Declare destination (vision, OKRs, KPIs), action steps, and whether steps were taken |
| **Coaching** | Declare current health (good and not good). For the not good: issue + proposed solution |
| **Transparency** | Share all relevant info (positive and negative). "Our imaginations are much more powerful than reality" -- actual data prevents worse assumptions |

### Conflict Resolution

1. Meet each party **separately** first (build trust in private)
2. Understand core issues without defensive posturing
3. When together: Person A shares thoughts and feelings fully
4. You summarize back until they say "that's right"
5. Only then present your response
6. Remain impartial -- goal is resolution, not victory

### Documentation System

**"If you do it twice, write it down."**

- Create company wiki mandatory for all new hires
- One process documented per week minimum
- Link all processes to master spreadsheet
- Require 3 months of use before allowing modifications
- Prevents knowledge loss through turnover

## Component 8: Sales & Fundraising

### Sales: Build Trust First

Before pitching:
1. Ask about their situation
2. Listen actively -- reflect back their words
3. At second meeting, reference first conversation details
4. Be explicit: "Before discussing us, I want to understand your situation"
5. Ask for limited time: "10-minute introductory call"

### Sell Results, Not Features

Customers care about business results, not functionality. Focus on "why" (the vision). Paint the picture of the world where their desires are fulfilled via your product.

### Three-Step Selling

1. Build trust with prospect
2. Identify customer's specific pain point
3. Present solution to THAT pain

### Sales Team Structure (Build ONLY at PMF)

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Qualifiers (SDRs)** | Generate qualified leads (separate inbound from outbound) |
| **Closers (AEs)** | Close qualified deals |
| **Farmers (CS)** | Retention + upsell |

Growth bottleneck is usually lead generation, not salespeople. Scale team only after predictable lead gen.

### Lead Generation: Seeds, Nets, Spears

- **Seeds:** Word-of-mouth, referrals, prior relationships (highest quality, slowest)
- **Nets:** Marketing-sourced -- events, SEO, ads (quantity over quality)
- **Spears:** Direct outbound -- email, LinkedIn (quality over quantity)

### Fundraising: Relationship First

- Pick a **partner investor**, not just a firm
- Get 3-5 network introductions via email (stack in same week)
- Build personal trust/liking BEFORE discussing company
- Sell yourself, not the company

**How to brag appropriately:**
1. Credit others' contributions
2. Highlight hard work required
3. Share personal vulnerability
4. Emphasize noble mission
5. Express gratitude

## Component 9: Vision & Strategy

### Ten-Year Vision Framework

Imagine dominant industry position and answer:
1. What industry do you dominate?
2. Who is your customer (real person, not company)?
3. What customer pain do you solve?
4. What makes your solution unique?
5. What competitive moat/asset do you control?

### Product-Market Fit

Know you've achieved it when:
- Long-term contracts (B2B)
- Second purchases / subscription renewals (B2C)
- High NPS
- Organic social sharing

**Team size rule:** Don't exceed 6 people before PMF. Premature scaling destroys startups.

### Focus Principle

Greatest startup risk = resource fragmentation. Concentrate on fewer priorities with depth. Startups fail from diffusion, not slowness.

### 50/50 Partnership Warning

Avoid equal splits in decision authority. Deadlocks harm momentum and culture. One person must be the tiebreaker.
